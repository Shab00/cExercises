If clouds gathered on the horizon, she would always bring her umbrella, just in case. 
It was a habit she had formed since childhood, when her mother told her that rain could arrive at any moment. 
The world around her felt uncertain, but she found comfort in these simple routines. 
She could wander the park for hours, watch people as they strolled by, and imagine stories about each one. 
Sometimes she would think about how things could have turned out differently, if she had chosen a different path. 
She wondered if she should reach out to old friends, or if she would only find memories that might sting. 
It was funny, she thought, how a single word could trigger so many recollections—and how a single cloud could darken or brighten a day depending on her mood.
